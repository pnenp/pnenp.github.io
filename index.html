2-PDS partition

A $2$-PDS partition of a graph $G=(V,E)$ is a partition $\Pi=\{C_1, C_2\}$ of $V$ such that $|C_i| \geq 2$ for each $C_i \in \Pi$ and $\forall i,j \in \{1,2\}$, $\forall u \in C_i$, \[ \frac{d_{C_i}(u)}{|C_i|-1} \geq \frac{d_{C_j}(u)}{|C_j|}\,. \] The elements of $\Pi$ are called PDS. A $2$-PDS partition can be found in polynomial time in trees, maximum degree $3$ and minimum degree $|V|-3$ (Bazgan, Chlebíková and Pontoizeau, 2018). However, the complexity of finding a $2$-PDS partition in general graphs remains unknown.

Proportionally dense subgraph (PDS)

Inspired by the above definition of a PDS partition, we define the notion a proportionally dense subgraph (PDS). Let $G=(V,E)$ be a graph, and $S \subset V$. We say that the induced subgraph $G[S]$ is a proportionally dense subgraph (PDS) if and only if $2 \leq |V| \lt S$ and for each vertex $u \in S$ $\frac{d_S(u)}{|S|-1} \geq \frac{ d(u)}{|V|-1}$, which is equivalent to $\frac{d_S(u)}{|S|-1} \geq \frac{ d_{\bar{S}}(u)}{|\bar{S}|}$. We say a vertex $u$ is satisfied (in $G[S]$) if it respects the above inequality. The size of the proportionally dense subgraph $G[S]$ corresponds to the cardinality of $S$.

Max Proportionally Dense Subgraph (Max PDS)
Input $G = (V,E)$ a graph, $k \in \mathbb{N}$.
Output A proportionally dense subgraph in $G$ of maximum size.

We prove that the problem is $\mathsf{APX}$-hard in split graphs and $\mathsf{NP}$-hard in bipartite graphs, and give a polynomial time $(2-\frac{2}{\Delta+1})$-approximation algorithm, with $\Delta$ the maximum degree in the graph (Bazgan, Chlebíková, Dallard, Pontoizeau, 2018).
